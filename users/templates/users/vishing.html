VISHING ATTACKS




What is VISHING attacks?


"Vishing" is a term used to describe a type of social engineering attack that is carried out over the telephone or VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) networks. The word "vishing" is a combination of "voice" and "phishing", which refers to a similar type of attack carried out via email.

The goal of vishing is to trick the victim into divulging sensitive information such as personal details, bank account information, credit card numbers, passwords, and other confidential data. The attacker usually poses as a legitimate authority figure, such as a bank representative, tech support agent, or law enforcement official, and creates a sense of urgency or fear to persuade the victim to provide the requested information.

Vishing attacks can take several forms, including:

  • Caller ID Spoofing: The attacker manipulates the caller ID to display a legitimate phone number, such as a bank's customer service line, to gain the victim's trust.
  • Interactive Voice Response (IVR) Spoofing: The attacker sets up a fake IVR system that mimics a legitimate one, to prompt the victim to provide sensitive information.
  • Voice Phishing: The attacker uses social engineering tactics to convince the victim to provide confidential information over the phone.


To protect against vishing attacks, individuals should be cautious when receiving unsolicited phone calls, verify the identity of the caller before providing any personal information, avoid sharing sensitive information over the phone, and report any suspicious activity to the relevant authorities.

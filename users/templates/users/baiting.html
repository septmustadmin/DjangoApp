PRE-TEXTING ATTACKS




What is PRE-TEXTING attacks?


"Baiting attacks" refer to a type of social engineering attack where an attacker uses a lure or bait to trick a victim into taking a certain action that is beneficial to the attacker. The goal of baiting attacks is to entice a victim with an offer that seems too good to be true or too enticing to pass up, and then manipulate the victim into performing an action that compromises their security.

One common example of a baiting attack is the use of fake USB drives. An attacker may leave a USB drive in a public place, such as a coffee shop or library, with a label indicating that it contains valuable information or a tempting label like "Company Payroll." When a victim picks up the USB drive and plugs it into their computer, it will automatically download malware that infects the system, giving the attacker access to sensitive information.

Another example of baiting attacks is the use of fake websites or online offers. An attacker may create a fake website that mimics a legitimate online retailer or financial institution, offering the victim an irresistible discount or free product in exchange for their personal information. Once the victim provides their information, the attacker can use it for malicious purposes, such as identity theft or unauthorized access to the victim's accounts.

To avoid becoming a victim of baiting attacks, it is essential to be cautious when presented with unexpected offers or lures. Never plug in or open files from unknown sources, and always verify the legitimacy of websites and offers before providing personal information or making a purchase.

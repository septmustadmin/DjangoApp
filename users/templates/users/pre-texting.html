PRE-TEXTING ATTACKS




What is PRE-TEXTING attacks?


Pretexting is a form of social engineering in which an attacker creates a fabricated scenario to trick someone into providing sensitive information or access to a system. The attacker assumes a false identity or pretext to gain the victim's trust and may use various techniques such as persuasion, impersonation, or manipulation to achieve their goal.

For example, an attacker might pose as a customer service representative or a law enforcement officer and use the pretext of an urgent issue to obtain sensitive information such as passwords or financial data. They may also use pretexting to gain physical access to a building or system by posing as a repair technician or maintenance worker.

Pretexting can be used in a variety of contexts, such as phishing attacks, social media scams, and identity theft. It is a common tactic used by cybercriminals to exploit human vulnerabilities and gain unauthorized access to systems and information.

To avoid falling victim to pretexting, it is important to be skeptical of unsolicited requests for information or access, verify the identity of anyone requesting sensitive information, and follow security protocols such as using strong passwords and two-factor authentication.

What is SEPTMUST?



Social Engineering Prevention Training Module for University Student (SEPTMUST) is a guide developed as a web-based application training module focusing on five type of social engineering technique which is Phishing, Vishing, Pre-Texting, Quid Pro Quo, and Baiting. SEPTMUST will be incorporated with videos, slides, minigames, quizzes to help increase the user understanding of social engineering. As social engineering becomes one of the highest success rate attacks in the world, SEPTMUS are developed to help lower the success rate of the attacks.



What is Social Engineering



Social engineering is the use of psychological manipulation and deception to influence people into taking certain actions or revealing confidential information. It involves exploiting human weaknesses such as trust, curiosity, fear, and greed to gain access to information or systems that would normally be inaccessible.

Social engineering attacks can take many forms, including phishing emails, pretexting, baiting, and quid pro quo tactics. In a phishing attack, for example, an attacker may send an email that appears to be from a trusted source such as a bank or a social media site, asking the recipient to click on a link or provide personal information. Pretexting involves creating a false pretext or story to gain access to sensitive information, while baiting involves leaving a tempting object, such as a USB drive, in a public place in the hope that someone will pick it up and use it.

Social engineering attacks can be highly effective, as they exploit human psychology and emotions rather than technical vulnerabilities. To protect against social engineering, it is important to be aware of common tactics and to be cautious when receiving unsolicited requests for information or actions. It is also important to implement security measures such as two-factor authentication and to educate employees about the risks of social engineering attacks.
